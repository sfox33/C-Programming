<h2 class="oneClick-disabled">Word Origin & History</h2>
<div>
<p>
letter mid-12c., "graphic symbol, written character," from O.Fr. lettre, from L. littera (also litera) "letter of the alphabet," of uncertain origin, perhaps from Gk. diphthera "tablet," with change of d- to l- as in lachrymose. In this sense it replaced O.E. bocst&aelig;f, lit. "book staff" (cf. Ger. Buchstabe "letter, character," from O.H.G. buohstab, from P.Gmc. *bok-staba-m). The pl. litter&aelig; in L. meant "epistle, written documents, literature," a sense first attested early 13c. in M.E., replacing O.E. &aelig;rendgewrit, lit. "errand-writing." School letter in sports, first awarded by <span class="hide" style="display: inline">...</span><span class="hide">U. of Chicago football coach Amos Alonzo Stagg. </span></p>
